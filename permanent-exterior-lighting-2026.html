Pre-Summer 2026 Edition | Permanent Programmable Lighting for Crested Butte Homes

Your Christmas Lights Aren't Really Christmas Lights

In Crested Butte, your Christmas lights aren't really Christmas lights. They go up before Thanksgiving and they don't come down until ski season ends and the weather finally lets someone get back up to the roof to take them down, usually in May. That's roughly six months of strands installed on your home. Whether they're lit on any given evening is up to you, but the strands themselves are committed.

For most owners, what's up there is whatever a holiday lighting service brought up the ladder in October. White strands along the eaves, maybe a wrapped aspen out front, the same scene as the house next door. It works, it's pleasant enough, it fits the cozy mountain-village aesthetic that draws people here in the first place. But the look on your roof was decided by someone else.

This edition is about the alternative: permanent programmable exterior lighting. It's installed once, integrated into your home's architecture, and yours to control. You decide what color it is, when it's on, and what it does. Default state is off. Designed for your specific home, ready for the many nights a year when you want the home to mark a particular occasion.

What It Actually Is, In Plain Terms

A low-profile aluminum track is installed along your fascia, soffits, and rooflines. Each segment houses individually-addressable LEDs that can be any color or any white temperature. The track is color-matched to your trim, so during the day it disappears. At night, it does whatever you tell it to do.

Up close in daylight you can see the fixtures sitting flush along the trim. From the driveway or the street, the track reads as part of the architecture.

What this product offers that no other exterior fixture can:

  • •Color and color temperature on demand. Other exterior fixtures are one color forever. This track is whatever you want for the occasion.
  • •Coordinated programming across the entire roofline. Separate fixtures can't talk to each other. This system runs as one composition.
  • •Animation when it's warranted. Slow fades for a wedding. A subtle warm shimmer for the holidays. Off when you don't want it.
  • •Permanent install, on-demand expression. No vendor scrambles. No ladders in November. You trigger the scene from your phone, your caretaker triggers it for an arrival.

Why Most Permanent Lighting Doesn't Survive a Winter Up Here

If you've tried a permanent lighting kit from Amazon or a national big-box brand, there's a good chance you've already watched it fail. The freeze-thaw cycles, the UV at nine thousand feet, the dump-and-melt of spring snow, the wind that comes down off the mountain in February. These are real conditions that destroy hardware engineered somewhere milder.

The product we install is designed and manufactured in Colorado, by a company that built it specifically for these conditions. It's UL-listed, rated for 100,000 hours of operation, and warrantied for seven years. The control hardware sits inside the home, where the weather can't reach it.

How It Actually Gets Used

Looking at our clients' actual scene programming, the system spends most of its life doing one thing and is used for the others on demand:

  • –Most of ski season: a low warm-white wash, intentionally tuned to the home, scheduled to come on at dusk
  • –Long Crested Butte summer evenings on the deck, with a low warm-white wash
  • –Christmas through New Year: a designed color program for the weeks when it means something
  • –A daughter's outdoor wedding or the rehearsal dinner on the deck
  • –The Fourth of July, when the family is in for the parade
  • –A milestone birthday or anniversary the family flies in for
  • –A college football Saturday, or your team's playoff run in January
  • –A welcome scene the caretaker triggers for arriving guests after dark

When We Can Actually Install

In the Gunnison Valley, the install window for permanent exterior lighting runs from roughly mid-May through mid-October. Outside that range, weather makes the work unpredictable, impractical, and at times unsafe.

If you want the system in place for the Fourth of July or a family wedding in August, the design conversation needs to happen now. If you want it in place for the holidays, our late-summer and fall calendar fills first with referrals from clients we worked with that summer.

The Bottom Line

Six months of the year, your roof has a strand installation on it whether you light it or not, and what's up there was decided by the holiday lighting service. Permanent programmable exterior lighting replaces the strands with hardware that's invisible by day and yours to control at night.

On for the holidays. On for a family arrival. Off the rest of the time, with nothing visible on the roof to show it. Designed for your specific home, not pulled off a shelf.
