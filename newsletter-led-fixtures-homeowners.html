Summer in Crested Butte is something special, whether you’re here year-round or arriving to open up your home for the season. Either way, spring is a good time to take care of a few things before the busy months hit.

One area that often gets overlooked is lighting. Fixtures age gradually, so it’s easy to miss how much they’ve changed over the years. We work with a lot of homeowners in the valley on lighting upgrades, and spring is the ideal window to get it done.

A Quick Look at What’s Changed in Home Lighting

A lot has changed in lighting over the past decade. If your fixtures are 10 to 15 years old, they’re still working, but the technology and design have come a long way since then. Newer LED fixtures produce better light, use far less energy, and last dramatically longer.

Swapping in new LED bulbs is a start, but the fixture itself matters more than most people realize. An outdated fixture limits how good the light can be, no matter what bulb you put in it.

Two Ways a Lighting Refresh Shows Up

Full-Time Residents

If you live here year-round, your lighting works hard. Long winter evenings, early sunsets, and daily wear of a mountain climate take a toll. A refresh changes how your home feels day to day.
 

Seasonal Homeowners

Arriving this summer after months away? Updated lighting is one of the easiest ways to walk into a home that feels fresh and well-maintained. Also helps if you ever rent or plan to list.
 

Where Lighting Makes the Biggest Difference

Not every fixture in the house needs attention. These are the spaces where upgrades tend to have the most impact.

Kitchen. This is where good lighting matters most. Proper task lighting over counters and islands makes cooking easier and the whole space feels brighter and more inviting.

Bathrooms. Outdated bathroom fixtures can make even expensive tile and finishes look dull. Modern fixtures with good color rendering bring those finishes back to life.

Living areas. Layered lighting in living rooms and great rooms creates warmth and flexibility. It’s the difference between a space that feels flat and one that feels like home.

Exterior & outdoor. Outdoor fixtures take the hardest beating from our mountain weather. Updated exterior lighting improves safety, curb appeal, and how you enjoy your outdoor spaces in the evenings.

Modern LED fixtures don’t need bulb replacements. That means no more keeping spare bulbs around, no more hard-to-reach fixtures that go dark for weeks, and lower utility bills year-round.

A typical whole-home fixture upgrade runs $8,000 to $20,000 depending on the size of the home. It’s a capital improvement that increases property value while reducing ongoing maintenance costs.
 

Good for Your Home, Good for Crested Butte

Living in Crested Butte comes with a shared commitment to taking care of this place. Modern LED fixtures cut lighting energy use by 75–85%. Fixtures that last two decades mean far less waste going to the landfill. It’s a simple upgrade that lines up with how most of us think about our impact here.
