Acoustics are critical for true cinema performance. Our approach includes:

Room Acoustics (Internal Sound Quality):

  • Absorption: Acoustic panels on walls/ceiling to eliminate echoes and reflections
  • Diffusion: Scatter reflections for natural, spacious sound
  • Bass Traps: Corner and wall treatments to control low-frequency resonance
  • Speaker Placement: Optimized positions based on room dimensions
  • Calibration: Professional tuning with measurement microphones

Soundproofing (Isolation from Other Spaces):

  • Wall Construction: Double/staggered-stud walls with mass-loaded vinyl
  • Ceiling/Floor Decoupling: Resilient channels, floating floors
  • Door Seals: Solid-core doors with acoustic seals
  • HVAC Silencing: Lined ducts, sound baffles, quiet equipment

Cost Impact: Basic acoustic treatment: $5,000-15,000. Professional soundproofing: $15,000-50,000+ depending on room size and isolation goals.

Both have strengths—the right choice depends on your space and priorities:

Projector + Screen (True Cinema Experience):

Pros:

  • • Massive screen sizes (100"-200"+)
  • • True theatrical immersion
  • • Acoustically transparent screens (speakers behind screen)
  • • Better for dark rooms (optimal viewing environment)
  • • More cinematic "feel"

Cons:

  • • Requires light control (dedicated theater or blackout shades)
  • • Lamp replacement every 2,000-4,000 hours ($200-500)
  • • Setup complexity (calibration, maintenance)

Large TV (MicroLED/OLED 85"-100"+):

Pros:

  • • Works in rooms with ambient light
  • • No lamp replacements (10+ year lifespan)
  • • Simpler setup and operation
  • • Perfect blacks (OLED)
  • • Better for multi-purpose media rooms

Cons:

  • • Limited to ~100" (affordable), ~146" (luxury MicroLED $100K+)
  • • Less immersive than larger screens
  • • Can't place speakers behind screen

Our Recommendation: Projector for dedicated theaters with light control. Large TV for multi-purpose media rooms with windows.

Timeline depends on project scope and whether it's new construction or retrofit:

New Construction (Ideal Scenario):

  • Design & Planning: 2-4 weeks (room layout, equipment selection)
  • Pre-Wire Phase: 3-5 days during framing (in-wall speakers, conduit, wiring)
  • Wait for Finishes: Weeks/months (drywall, paint, flooring by builder)
  • Equipment Install: 5-10 days (speakers, projector, rack, seating)
  • Programming & Calibration: 2-3 days (audio tuning, video calibration, control system)
  • Training & Handoff: Half-day walkthrough
  • Total Active Time: 2-3 weeks (spread over construction timeline)

Existing Space Retrofit:

  • Design & Planning: 1-2 weeks
  • Construction (if needed): 1-3 weeks (acoustic treatment, soundproofing, finishes)
  • AV Installation: 1-2 weeks (all equipment, wiring, seating)
  • Calibration & Training: 2-3 days
  • Total Timeline: 4-8 weeks from start to finished theater
