Absolutely! Modern motorized shades are designed for easy retrofit installation:

Battery-Powered Shades (Easiest Retrofit):

  • • No electrical wiring needed—motors run on rechargeable batteries
  • • Install in existing window frames without modification
  • • Wireless control (RF remote, smartphone app)
  • • Battery life: 3-5 years with typical use (1-2 cycles per day)
  • • Recharge via USB or solar panel options
  • • Best for: Existing homes, no access to power

Hardwired Shades (Best Performance):

  • • Requires 110V power near each window
  • • More powerful motors for larger/heavier shades
  • • No battery maintenance or recharging
  • • We fish wires through walls/attic when possible
  • • Sometimes requires minor drywall patching
  • • Best for: Maximum performance, large shades, frequent use

Installation Process:

  • 1. Measure windows and select fabrics
  • 2. Custom-build shades to exact dimensions
  • 3. Install mounting brackets (inside or outside mount)
  • 4. Program and integrate with control system
  • 5. Typical timeline: 4-6 weeks from order to installation

We assess your windows during consultation and recommend the best retrofit approach.

Battery life depends on shade size, usage frequency, and battery type:

Typical Battery Life:

  • Standard Use (1-2 cycles/day): 3-5 years before recharge
  • Light Use (few times/week): 5-7 years
  • Heavy Use (4+ cycles/day): 1-3 years

Battery Types:

  • Lithium-ion rechargeable: Most common, USB rechargeable, 500+ charge cycles
  • Alkaline disposable: Some budget systems, requires periodic replacement
  • Solar-powered: Trickle-charge from sunlight, extends battery life significantly

Low Battery Warnings:

  • • Shades notify you via app when battery is low (usually 20% remaining)
  • • Plenty of time to recharge before shades stop working
  • • Manual operation still works if battery dies

Recharging:

  • • USB charging wand (connects to bottom of shade)
  • • Full recharge: 4-8 hours
  • • Can recharge without removing shade from window

Hardwired Alternative: For shades used heavily (media rooms, conference rooms), hardwired motors eliminate battery concerns entirely.

Fabric selection depends on room function, light control needs, and aesthetics:

Sheer/Solar Shades (Daytime Privacy + Light):

  • • Openness: 1%-14% (higher % = more view, less privacy)
  • • UV protection: 90-99% (protects furniture, reduces glare)
  • • View-through: See outside, maintain soft natural light
  • • Best for: Living rooms, offices, kitchens during the day
  • • Note: Not for nighttime privacy (lights visible from outside)

Room-Darkening (95-99% Light Block):

  • • Blocks most light but not 100%
  • • Some light leakage around edges
  • • Better insulation than sheers
  • • Best for: Bedrooms, media rooms (moderate light control)

Blackout (100% Light Block):

  • • Complete darkness when closed
  • • Side channels seal light gaps
  • • Opaque backing material
  • • Best for: Home theaters, master bedrooms, media rooms

Specialty Materials:

  • Hartmann & Forbes Natural Wovens: Grass, bamboo, reeds—organic texture and warmth
  • Dual Shades: Sheer + blackout on one roller—switch between view and privacy
  • Fabrics + Drapery: Layer motorized shades behind manual drapes for aesthetics

Our Consultation Process:

  • • Visit your home with fabric samples
  • • Assess light direction, window size, room function
  • • Show how different fabrics look in your actual lighting
  • • Recommend optimal solutions for each room

Different fabrics for different rooms is common—theater gets blackout, living room gets sheers, bedrooms get room-darkening.
