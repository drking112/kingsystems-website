Landscape Lighting for Mountain Homes

Your Crested Butte property has incredible outdoor spaces. Whether it's a stone patio, a mature aspen grove, or mountain views from the deck, the right landscape lighting transforms these spaces from daylight-only enjoyment to something you can use well into the evening.

Spring is the ideal time to plan and install landscape lighting. The ground is workable, installation crews can move efficiently, and you'll have the system ready for summer entertaining and fall evenings.

What Good Landscape Lighting Does

Professional landscape lighting isn't about making your yard look like a stadium. It's about creating layers of light that enhance the spaces you already use and love:

Accent Key Features

Uplighting mature trees, highlighting stone walls, washing light across architectural details that define your property.

Path & Safety Lighting

Gentle pathway lighting that guides movement without glare, making outdoor spaces safe and inviting after dark.

Outdoor Living Areas

Soft ambient lighting for decks, patios, and outdoor dining areas that extends your usable outdoor season.

Security & Peace of Mind

Strategic lighting around entry points and dark corners, especially important for homes that sit empty part of the year.

Why Spring Installation Makes Sense

The installation window for landscape lighting in the Gunnison Valley is practical from late April through October. Spring installation gives you several advantages:

  • •Ground conditions are ideal for running low-voltage cable and setting fixtures
  • •Installation happens before your peak summer use of the property
  • •You'll have the system fully tested and tuned before fall gatherings
  • •Spring calendars book faster than summer due to construction schedules

What Makes a System Last Up Here

Mountain weather is hard on outdoor fixtures. Cheap box-store systems fail within a few seasons. Professional-grade landscape lighting is built differently:

Solid Brass or Copper Fixtures

Won't corrode or crack in freeze-thaw cycles. Age gracefully with a natural patina.

LED Technology

20+ year lifespan, minimal heat, consistent color over time. Far superior to halogen.

Low Voltage System

Safe, energy-efficient, easy to adjust. Transformer typically lives indoors where weather can't reach it.

Proper Cable Burial

Direct-burial cable rated for ground contact. Won't fail from moisture or rodent damage.

Smart Control Options

Modern landscape lighting systems can be automated and controlled remotely:

  • –Dusk-to-dawn sensors that adjust with the seasons
  • –Programmable timers that turn lights off at a set time
  • –Smartphone control via WiFi (turn on lights for a caretaker, trigger before arrival)
  • –Integration with whole-home control systems if you have one

What It Costs

Professional landscape lighting typically runs:

  • •$4,000 to $8,000 for a focused installation (entry, patio, and a few key trees)
  • •$10,000 to $18,000 for comprehensive property lighting
  • •$20,000+ for large estates with extensive landscapes

Operating costs are minimal—most systems run under $10/month in electricity. LED fixtures last 20+ years with zero maintenance.
